# Glossary

This glossary provides definitions and explanations for key terms used throughout the SCAR (Supply Chain Anomaly Radar) system documentation.

---

## A

**AI (Artificial Intelligence)**
Computer systems able to perform tasks that normally require human intelligence, such as understanding natural language, recognizing patterns, and making decisions.

**Alert**
Automated notification triggered when specified conditions are met (e.g., KPI threshold exceeded).

**Anomaly**
A deviation from expected patterns or norms, often indicating a potential issue or opportunity.

---

## B

**Backwardation**
Market condition where future prices are lower than current prices, typically indicating tight supply.

**Ballast**
A vessel traveling empty without cargo.

**Baltic Dry Index (BDI)**
An index measuring the cost of transporting dry bulk commodities such as coal, grain, and iron ore by sea.

**Barrel (Bbl)**
Standard unit of volume for crude oil and petroleum products, equal to 42 US gallons or approximately 159 liters.

**Basis**
The difference between the spot price and futures price of a commodity.

**Beginning Stocks**
Inventory carried over from the previous period.

**Brent Crude**
A major trading classification of crude oil from the North Sea, used as a benchmark for oil pricing.

**Bunker Fuel**
Heavy fuel oil used by ships for propulsion.

**Bushel**
A unit of volume used for agricultural commodities (approximately 35.24 liters for wheat).

---

## C

**Capesize**
The largest category of dry bulk vessel, typically over 100,000 DWT, too large for Panama Canal.

**Carryover Stocks**
Inventory held from previous production cycle.

**CFI (Containerized Freight Index)**
Index measuring container shipping rates on major trade routes.

**Charter Rate**
The cost to hire a vessel for a specified period.

**Commodity**
A basic good or raw material that is interchangeable with other commodities of the same type (e.g., wheat, crude oil, gold).

**Concentration Risk**
Risk arising from over-dependence on a limited number of suppliers or sources.

**Contango**
Market condition where future prices are higher than current prices, normal for storable commodities.

**CRB Index (Commodity Research Bureau Index)**
A broad measure of commodity market performance tracking 19 commodities across four sectors.

**Crush**
The processing of oilseeds (like soybeans) into meal and oil.

**cwt**
Hundredweight, a unit of measurement equal to 100 pounds (approximately 45.4 kg).

---

## D

**Dashboard**
A visual display of key data and metrics, organized for quick comprehension and monitoring.

**Data Lag**
The time delay between when an event occurs and when data about it becomes available.

**Deadweight Tonnage (DWT)**
Maximum cargo weight a vessel can safely carry.

**Demand**
The quantity of a commodity that consumers are willing and able to purchase at various prices.

**Demurrage**
Fees charged for delaying a vessel beyond the agreed time for loading or unloading.

**Diesel**
A type of fuel oil used in diesel engines, commonly for transportation and industrial applications.

---

## E

**Ending Stocks**
Inventory remaining at the end of a reporting period.

**EU (European Union)**
An economic and political union of European countries.

**Export**
Goods or services produced in one country and sold to another country.

**Export Ban**
Government prohibition on exporting specific commodities.

---

## F

**Feed Use**
Commodity consumption as animal feed.

**Food Security**
Assured access to sufficient, safe, and nutritious food.

**Food Use**
Commodity consumption for human consumption.

**Forecast**
A prediction of future events or trends based on analysis of historical data and current conditions.

**Freight**
Goods transported by truck, train, ship, or aircraft, or the charge for such transportation.

**Futures Price**
The price agreed upon for delivery of a commodity at a specified future date.

---

## G

**Gasoline**
A refined petroleum product used as fuel in internal combustion engines.

**Gross Ton**
A unit of volume equal to 100 cubic feet (approximately 2.83 cubic meters).

---

## H

**Harmonized System (HS) Code**
International standardized system of names and numbers to classify traded products.

**Heat Map**
A data visualization technique using color to represent values in a matrix.

**Herfindahl-Hirschman Index (HHI)**
A measure of market concentration calculated by squaring market shares and summing the results.

**HS Code**
See Harmonized System Code.

---

## I

**Import**
Goods or services brought into a country from abroad.

**Import Quota**
A government-imposed limit on the quantity of a commodity that can be imported.

**Index**
A statistical measure of changes in a portfolio of stocks, commodities, or other assets.

**Industrial Use**
Commodity consumption for non-food purposes (e.g., ethanol from corn).

---

## J

**Jet Fuel**
A type of aviation turbine fuel used in aircraft turbine engines.

**JODI (Joint Organisations Data Initiative)**
A collaborative effort to improve the availability and reliability of energy statistics.

---

## K

**Kilogram (kg)**
Metric unit of mass, equal to 1,000 grams or approximately 2.2 pounds.

**KPI (Key Performance Indicator)**
A measurable value demonstrating how effectively an objective is being achieved.

---

## L

**Laytime**
The agreed time allocated for loading or unloading cargo at a port.

**Liner**
A vessel operating on a fixed route with scheduled departures.

**LPG (Liquefied Petroleum Gas)**
Mixture of propane and butane gases, liquefied for ease of transport and storage.

---

## M

**Metric Ton (MT)**
Unit of mass equal to 1,000 kilograms or approximately 2,205 pounds.

**MoM (Month-over-Month)**
Comparison of a value to the same value in the immediate prior month.

---

## N

**NEFA (National Emergency Food Authority)**
See National Emergency Authority.

**Net Weight**
Weight of goods excluding packaging materials.

**Nimbus**
Israeli government cloud infrastructure used by SCAR system.

---

## O

**Oilseed**
Seeds and crops grown primarily for the oil contained in their seeds (soybeans, sunflower, canola).

---

## P

**Panamax**
Category of vessel sized to fit through the Panama Canal, typically 60,000-80,000 DWT.

**Production**
The total quantity of a commodity produced in a given period.

**Propane**
A hydrocarbon gas used as fuel for heating, cooking, and vehicles.

---

## Q

**QoQ (Quarter-over-Quarter)**
Comparison of a value to the same value in the immediately preceding quarter.

**Quota**
A government-imposed limit on the quantity of a commodity that can be produced, exported, or imported.

---

## R

**RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation)**
An AI technique that enhances responses by retrieving relevant information from a database before generating answers.

**Re-export**
Export of previously imported goods without substantial transformation.

**Risk-News**
News articles classified in shortage categories (excluding "neutral"), representing supply chain risks.

---

## S

**Sanctions**
Economic penalties imposed by countries or international bodies to influence behavior.

**SCAR (Supply Chain Anomaly Radar)**
The Israeli supply chain monitoring and risk assessment system.

**Sentiment Analysis**
The use of AI to identify and extract subjective information from text (positive, negative, neutral).

**Spot Market**
Market for immediate delivery of commodities, as opposed to futures contracts.

**Spot Price**
Current market price for immediate delivery of a commodity.

**Stock-to-Use Ratio**
The ratio of ending stocks to total use, indicating market tightness.

**Substitution**
Replacement of one commodity with another similar commodity.

**Supramax**
Category of dry bulk vessel, typically 50,000-60,000 DWT.

**Supply**
The quantity of a commodity that producers are willing and able to sell at various prices.

**Supply Chain**
The network of organizations, people, activities, and resources involved in moving a product from supplier to customer.

---

## T

**Tariff**
A tax or duty imposed on imported or exported goods.

**TBPD (Thousand Barrels Per Day)**
Unit of measurement for oil production and consumption.

**TEU (Twenty-foot Equivalent Unit)**
Standard unit of measure for container capacity.

**Total Supply**
Production plus beginning stocks plus imports.

**Trade Barrier**
Government-imposed restriction on international trade (tariffs, quotas, regulations).

**Tramp**
A vessel that does not operate on a fixed schedule or route.

**Transit Trade**
Trade where goods pass through a country without being consumed there.

---

## U

**UN Comtrade**
United Nations Commodity Trade Statistics Database.

**Urea**
A nitrogen-containing compound used as fertilizer.

**USDA (United States Department of Agriculture)**
US federal agency providing agricultural statistics and forecasts.

---

## V

**Volatility**
The degree of variation in a commodity's price over time.

---

## W

**WASDE (World Agricultural Supply and Demand Estimates)**
Monthly report from USDA forecasting supply and demand for major crops.

**Week-over-Week (WoW)**
Comparison of a value to the same value in the immediately preceding week.

**WTI (West Texas Intermediate)**
A grade of crude oil used as a benchmark for oil pricing in the Americas.

---

## Y

**Yield**
The amount of a crop produced per unit area (e.g., bushels per acre, tonnes per hectare).

**Year-over-Year (YoY)**
Comparison of a value to the same value in the year-ago period.
